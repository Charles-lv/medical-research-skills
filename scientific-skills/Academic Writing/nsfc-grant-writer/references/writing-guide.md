## General Principles

- Total length: ≤30 pages. Aim for ~25 pages of substantive content.
- Language: Simplified Chinese throughout. Scientific terms may retain English in parentheses on first use.
- Tone: Formal academic Chinese. Avoid first-person singular (); use Application or .
- All section headings from the template must be preserved exactly.
- Sections marked with `[ ]` in the template are the content areas to fill in.
- Sections NOT marked with `[ ]` (Working Conditions, in , Completed Projects, Part IV) are left as headings only — do NOT generate content for them.
- **Citation rule**: Literature citations `[N]` are ONLY permitted in Section I (Research Rationale). The abstract, Section II, Section III, and Section IV must contain NO citations whatsoever.

## Abstract

**Structure**: Chinese abstract + English abstract + keywords

**Chinese abstract** (~400 characters):
- Background (1-2 sentences): disease/problem significance
- Gap/problem (1 sentence): what is unknown
- Hypothesis (1 sentence): the scientific hypothesis
- Approach (2-3 sentences): key research modules
- Expected outcomes (1 sentence)

**IMPORTANT**: Do NOT include any literature citations (`[N]`) in the abstract. The abstract is citation-free.

**English abstract** (~200 words): parallel structure to Chinese abstract

**Keywords**: 5-8 terms, Chinese and English pairs, separated by semicolons

**Example structure**:
```
【Abstract】
[Chinese abstract text]

【Abstract】
[English abstract text]

【Keywords】Keywords1；Keywords2；Keywords3
【Keywords】keyword1; keyword2; keyword3
```

## Section I: Research Rationale (Research Rationale)

**Target length**: ~8-10 pages

**Required structure**: 5 numbered subsections with subheadings, followed by the hypothesis statement block and reference list.

Use `####` level headings for each subsection. Example:

```
#### 1. {/} and Significance
#### 2. {/} in {} in Research
#### 3. {Keywords} and {} 
#### 4. {/} Research and not 
#### 5. { for Hypothesis Analysis or }
```

Generate subheading titles that accurately reflect the content of each subsection based on the hypothesis.

**Preliminary data integration rule**: Do NOT create a dedicated "Preliminary Research Foundation" subsection. Instead, weave the applicant's preliminary findings naturally into whichever subsections they are most relevant to. For example:
- If preliminary data shows expression of a key molecule in clinical samples → mention it within the subsection discussing that molecule's role
- If bioinformatics analysis links two molecules → embed it in the subsection covering that relationship
- Each piece of preliminary data should appear as 1-3 sentences within the relevant subsection, introduced as "ApplicationResearch..." or "ApplicationTeam......"
- Reference figures/tables as (Figure1)(Figure2)(Table1) etc. within the running text
- Distribute preliminary data across at least 2-3 different subsections to show breadth of prior work

If user only provided the hypothesis (no prior data), generate plausible preliminary work distributed across subsections:
- Clinical sample expression analysis of key molecules (Figure1) — embed in the subsection about that molecule
- Bioinformatics analysis, e.g., TCGA/GEO dataset mining (Figure2) — embed in the subsection about the pathway/correlation
- In vitro functional validation (Figure3) — embed in the subsection about the mechanism
- Correlation with clinical parameters (Table1) — embed in the subsection about clinical significance

### Subsection 1: Research Significance (ResearchSignificance)
- Open with disease/field epidemiology and clinical/social burden (incidence, mortality, 5-year survival, etc.)
- State the current treatment landscape and its limitations
- State the unmet need or knowledge gap that motivates this project
- Cite 1-2 PubMed references
- ~400-500 characters

### Subsections 2-5: Literature Review (with integrated preliminary data)
- Each subsection covers one thematic area, progressing from broad background → specific molecular/pathway context → direct relevance to hypothesis
- Each subsection must cite 2-4 PubMed references using inline `[N]` notation
- Subsection 2: broad molecular/pathway background relevant to the hypothesis
- Subsection 3: specific mechanism or interaction proposed in the hypothesis
- Subsection 4: current knowledge gaps, contradictions, or unanswered questions that the hypothesis addresses
- Subsection 5: synthesis or key unresolved question that the hypothesis directly answers; may include the most compelling preliminary finding
- ~500-700 characters per subsection (longer if preliminary data is embedded)

### Scientific Hypothesis Statement
After subsection 5, write (as plain paragraphs, no subheading):
> to Analysis，ApplicationHypothesis：[restate the hypothesis clearly and formally]

Then write the research direction overview:
> is ValidationProjectHypothesis， from to Research：[list 3-4 research directions in 1-2 sentences each]

### Reference List
- Placed at the end of Section I under heading `#### References`
- APA format (see Citation Format section)
- Number sequentially [1], [2], [3]...
- Include all references cited in Section I

## Section II: Research Content (Research Content)

**Target length**: ~8-10 pages

### 1. Research Content Modules

Design 3-4 modules with logical progression:
- Module 1: Establish/confirm the phenomenon (Validation)
- Module 2: Investigate the mechanism ()
- Module 3: Functional validation / intervention (Validation/)
- Module 4 (optional): Translational/clinical relevance (Research)

Each module format:
```
（N）[Module title — concise, action-oriented]
a. [sub-item]
b. [sub-item]
...
```

Sub-items (a, b, c, ...) should be determined by the actual content of each module — use as many as needed to fully describe the research plan. Simple modules may have 2-3 sub-items; complex modules may have 4-5. Each sub-item describes one specific experiment, approach, or expected result. The last sub-item of each module should state the expected results and their significance.

### 2. Innovation Points ( and )

3-4 bullet points. Each innovation should be:
- Specific and verifiable (not vague claims like "Research")
- Tied to a concrete aspect of the project
- ~50-80 characters each

Format:
```
（1）[Innovation point 1]
（2）[Innovation point 2]
（3）[Innovation point 3]
```

### 3. Annual Research Plan (Research)

4-year plan for :

| Year | Focus | Key deliverables |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| | Foundation/validation | Key molecules confirmed, models established |
| | Mechanism investigation | Core pathway elucidated |
| | Functional validation | In vivo/intervention studies |
| | Integration/translation | Synthesis, manuscript submission |

Write as prose paragraphs, not a table.

## Section III: Research Foundation (Research)

**Target length**: ~4-6 pages

### 1.1 Prior Research Foundation (Preliminary Research Foundation)

**CRITICAL**: Must be consistent with and expand upon the preliminary data described in Section I.

For each item mentioned in Section I (Figure1, Figure2, Figure3, Table1, etc.), write an expanded paragraph:
- What was done (methods)
- What was found (results, with specific data if possible)
- Why it supports the hypothesis

Format:
```
（1）[Title of prior work item]
[Expanded description, 200-400 characters]

（2）[Title of prior work item]
[Expanded description]
```

### 1.2 Feasibility Analysis (Feasibility Analysis)

Address 3-4 dimensions:
- ****：hypothesis is well-supported by literature and preliminary data
- **Technical Roadmap**：methods are established and validated in the lab
- **ResearchTeam should **：team has relevant expertise (generic statement)
- ** and should for **：potential risks and mitigation strategies

~400-600 characters total

## Citation Format

### Inline citations
- Single reference: `[1]`
- Two references at same location: `[1][2]`
- Never cite more than 2 references at a single location
- Place citation immediately after the relevant statement, before punctuation

### Reference list format (APA)
```
[1] Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx

[2] Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
```

Rules:
- List all authors (up to 6; if more, list first 6 then "et al.")
- Journal names in full (not abbreviated)
- Include DOI when available
- Number sequentially in order of first appearance in text
